Michael G's Amateur Landscape Photography Tips

 

Not Too Symmetric

Having some asymmetry can make your picture more interesting. In this case, the palm tree is not quite in the center.

Find Pathways

A trail, road, or river curving into the distance gives the viewer something to follow visually.

Forming a Frame

Trees or buildings can help form a frame that emphasizes whatever is in the middle of the picture.

Show the Clouds

Don't forget to look up. If clouds are becoming dramatic, you can emphasize the sky above the landscape more than the landscape itself.

Give Lakes a Shore

Lakes usually look better if some of the near shore is visible. It helps viewers feel like they're standing there, as well as giving a bit of a frame to the water.

Use Scale Indicators

To show how big something is, include something with a recognizable size for comparison. Here, the tiny road in the distance helps to indicate the size of the valley.

About

       My name is Michael Grunow. I photograph scenery to preserve my memories of the beauty that I see, both for myself and for sharing with others. My interest in photography began when my parents gave me a video camera that could also take low-quality snapshots. After some time taking videos and photos of walks outdoors, I was given a better camera. I kept practicing, and over the years my skills have strengthened to the level that you see here.
